Exercise 2: Implement the Divide and Conquer algorithm to find the Strongly Connected Components in a directed graph.

 

Algorithm:

 

1.     The basic idea is to select an arbitrary vertex v, which we call a pivot vertex,

and find its descendant and predecessor sets.

2.     The intersection of the predecessor and descendant sets is SCC(G, v). After outputting SCC(G,v), the vertices in G \SCC(G, v) are divided into three sets: Desc(G, v), P red(G, v), and Rem(G, v).

3.     Any additional strongly connected component must be entirely contained within one of

these three sets, so we can divide the problem and recurse.

 

 

Average Time Complexity: O(ElogV) where V is No.of Vertices and E is No.of Edges.
